## 3. Blocker — the xlsx atom numbering does not match the logs

The analysis above had to match experimental shifts to computed atoms **by sorted
shift order**, not by the labels in `data_dJ_DP4.xlsx`, because those labels are in
a different numbering than the Gaussian/mol2 atom order. Evidence:

- In the logs, atoms **1–7** are sp²/carbonyl carbons (shielding 90–131 ppm) and
  atoms **9,10,11,12,14,15,16,17,19** are the 9 sp³ carbons (184–218 ppm).
- The experimental ¹³C shifts are all aliphatic (16.1–71.6 ppm), yet the xlsx
  labels them `1,2,3,4,5,6,7,…` — i.e. it puts aliphatic shifts on aromatic atoms.
- The ¹H labels are likewise inconsistent (label 16 → a carbon, 18 → an oxygen,
  13 → the nitrogen).
- Count mismatch: 10 experimental aliphatic ¹³C shifts vs 9 sp³ carbons in the
  structure (C₁₆H₁₉NO₂ → 7 sp² + 9 sp³). One extra experimental carbon.

Consequences of the current work-around:
- The ¹³C ranking is **assignment-free** (optimistic — it pairs sorted shifts), so
  the 93 % is an upper bound on confidence, not a rigorous DP4.
- **¹H was excluded entirely** (cannot be mapped at all).
- **DP4+ was not computed**: it additionally needs *unscaled* shifts (a TMS
  reference calculation at the same HF/STO-3G level) and level-matched parameters.
